Hydrogen–Lattice Quantum Computing Simulations

research-1.jpg
(diagram / figure) goes here

As part of the NSF Summer Research Opportunities Program (SROP), I joined the Mendoza-Cortés Group at Michigan State University, working under the mentorship of William Comaskey on the Hydrogen-Lattice-QC project.

The project investigates near-term quantum algorithms — specifically the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) and Quantum Imaginary Time Evolution (QITE) — for simulating the ground-state energies of hydrogen lattice systems. A significant part of the work also focused on quantum error mitigation techniques to make results from noisy, near-term quantum hardware more reliable.

  • VQE
  • QITE
  • Quantum Error Mitigation
  • Hydrogen Lattice Simulation

This work builds on the Hydrogen-Lattice-QC repository, maintained by the Mendoza-Cortés Group — not my own repo, credited here rather than hosted on my GitHub. View the repository (placeholder link).
